When Do You Need Building Regulations Approval?

Extensions & Additions

Single-storey, double-storey, and wrap-around extensions. Conservatories, orangeries, and outbuildings over certain sizes.

Loft Conversions

Converting loft spaces into habitable rooms, including dormer additions and structural alterations to roof spaces.

Structural Alterations

Removing or altering load-bearing walls, installing steel beams, or making significant structural modifications.

Electrical Work

New electrical installations, consumer unit replacements, and electrical work in kitchens, bathrooms, and gardens.

Heat Sources

Installing new boilers, wood burning stoves, oil tanks, and other heating appliances requiring building control approval.

New Buildings

All new construction including houses, garages, workshops, and commercial buildings require full building regulations approval.
